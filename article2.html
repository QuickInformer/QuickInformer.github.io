Modern Human System


Early Years of Life

We were born into this system. From the time you stepped into this world, there was a set way that your life would head to. You were put into a conditioning factory, where your mind would be programmed and given the type of functions they wanted it to possess. A very known place where this occurs is at regular school. If you pay close attention at the "educational system" you will notice that most things taught there is basically useless.

Grip on your Mind

Your mind is valuable. It's what tells you what you want. If you don't have control over your mind it's because something external has control or influence over your thoughts and mental state.

School Life

It's all a matter of time before you find yourself following the crowd's way of doing things. This affects us all, that's how they been doing it. Be born, learn to walk, go to school from kindergarden through highschool, spend around 7 hours of your 24 hours at school, plus 1 hour doing homework. Land a job while in highschool, responsibilities stacking up, graduate highschool. After done with highschool, you're technically on your own. Follow your aspirations, get a full time job, move out your parents/guardian house. Go to college, graduate college.

School as Conditioning Factory

Modern school education often emphasizes conformity and compliance rather than individuality and critical thinking. Issues include standardization and a focus on preparing students for existing roles, which can limit creativity. Treating students as if they exist only to fit into a mold instead of unique thinkers.

This approach not only undermines students' passions but also prepares them to fit into a system rather than challenging it. We need a change in education that empowers students to explore, innovate, and engage meaningfully with the world, nurturing their potentials as independent thinkers and leaders.

After your 60s, you retire and try to enjoy the painful rest of your life at an retirement center, thinking about your overall life decisions and experiences. The end.
